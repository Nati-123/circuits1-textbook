# Chapter 2: Ohm’s Law and Basic Circuit Configurations

## 2.1 Ohm’s Law

Ohm’s Law defines the relationship between voltage, current, and resistance:

$$
V = IR
$$

Where:
- V = Voltage (Volts)
- I = Current (Amps)
- R = Resistance (Ohms)

---

## 2.2 Power in Circuits

Power is given by:

$$
P = VI
$$

Also:

$$
P = I^2 R
$$

$$
P = \frac{V^2}{R}
$$

---

## 2.3 Series Circuits

- Same current flows through all elements

$$
R_{total} = R_1 + R_2 + R_3 + \dots
$$

---

## 2.4 Parallel Circuits

- Same voltage across all branches

$$
\frac{1}{R_{total}} = \frac{1}{R_1} + \frac{1}{R_2} + \dots
$$

---

## 2.5 Summary

- Ohm’s Law: \( V = IR \)
- Series: resistances add
- Parallel: reciprocals add
