# Chapter 1 — Electric Charge and Basic Circuit Quantities

## Introduction
This chapter introduces the fundamental electrical quantities used in circuit analysis.

---

## Electric Charge
Electric charge is the basic property of matter responsible for electrical phenomena.

\[
Q = It
\]

Where:
- \(Q\) = charge (Coulombs)
- \(I\) = current (Amperes)
- \(t\) = time (seconds)

---

## Current
Electric current is the rate of flow of charge.

\[
I = \frac{dQ}{dt}
\]

---

## Voltage
Voltage is the electric potential difference between two points.

---

## Power
Electrical power is the rate at which energy is transferred.

\[
P = VI
\]

---

## Resistance
Resistance is the opposition to the flow of current.

\[
R = \frac{V}{I}
\]

---

## Summary
- Charge is the foundation of electricity  
- Current is flow of charge  
- Voltage drives current  
- Resistance opposes current  
- Power represents energy transfer
